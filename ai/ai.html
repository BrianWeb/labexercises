Key AI Concepts

What Is a Large Language Model (LLM)?

Simple explanation

A Large Language Model is an AI system trained on very large amounts of text so that it can recognise patterns in language and generate likely next words, sentences, or responses.

  • It does not “understand” like a human in the full sense.
  • It predicts language based on patterns learned from training data.
  • It can be used for writing, summarising, coding, translation, and question answering.

Why it feels impressive

  • It has seen huge amounts of language examples.
  • It can generalise patterns across many domains.
  • It produces fluent responses very quickly.
Important point: An LLM is not simply storing whole books in a database and copying them back. It is learning statistical patterns in language.
Key AI Concepts

What Is Machine Learning?

Basic idea

Machine learning is when a computer system improves its performance by learning patterns from data rather than following only fixed hand-written rules.

Typical process

  • Collect data
  • Train a model
  • Test performance
  • Use the model on new cases

Examples

  • Spam filtering
  • Fraud detection
  • Demand forecasting
  • Recommendation systems
Simple contrast: Traditional programming tells the computer exactly what to do. Machine learning lets the computer discover useful patterns from examples.
Key AI Concepts

Training Data, Tokens, and Parameters

Training data

This is the information the model learns from, such as text, images, transactions, or sensor data.

Tokens

Tokens are small pieces of text that a language model processes, such as words, parts of words, or punctuation.

Parameters

Parameters are the internal numerical weights the model adjusts during training to learn patterns.

Useful analogy: Training data is like the reading material. Tokens are the chunks of language being processed. Parameters are the learned internal settings that shape the model’s behaviour.
Key AI Concepts

Generative AI vs Predictive AI

Generative AI

Generative AI creates new content based on patterns it has learned.

  • Writes text
  • Creates images
  • Generates code
  • Produces summaries

Predictive AI

Predictive AI estimates likely outcomes based on historical data.

  • Predicts demand
  • Flags fraud risk
  • Scores customer churn
  • Forecasts sales
Business point: Many firms were using predictive AI before the recent excitement around generative AI. ChatGPT made AI visible to the public, but AI in business has existed for years in forecasting, analytics, and automation.
Key AI Concepts

What Is a Neural Network?

Basic idea

A neural network is a mathematical system inspired loosely by the structure of the human brain. It learns patterns by adjusting internal numerical connections.

How learning happens

  • Input data enters the network
  • Signals pass through layers
  • Errors are measured
  • Connections are adjusted

Why they matter

Neural networks are the foundation of modern AI systems including image recognition, speech systems, and large language models.

Simple analogy: Like adjusting many small dials until a machine produces better answers.
Key AI Concepts

What Is Reinforcement Learning?

Core idea

Reinforcement learning allows AI systems to learn by trying actions and receiving rewards or penalties.

  • Good actions receive positive feedback
  • Bad actions receive negative feedback
  • The system gradually improves its strategy

Examples

  • Game-playing AI (AlphaGo)
  • Robot movement learning
  • Recommendation optimisation
  • Dynamic pricing systems
Think: How is this similar to how humans learn skills like driving or playing sport?
Key AI Concepts

AI Hallucinations and Limitations

What is a hallucination?

Sometimes AI systems generate confident but incorrect information because they are predicting likely language patterns rather than checking verified facts.

Why this happens

  • Training data gaps
  • Ambiguous questions
  • Over-generalisation
  • Lack of real-world grounding
Business risk: Managers must treat AI outputs as decision support — not unquestionable truth.
Key AI Concepts

Bias and Data Quality

Bias risk

AI systems can learn patterns that reflect historical inequality or poor decision-making.

Data quality

Inaccurate or incomplete data can lead to poor predictions and flawed recommendations.

Good practice

  • Human oversight
  • Testing and auditing
  • Diverse datasets
  • Clear accountability
Discussion: Should businesses rely on AI decisions if they cannot fully explain how the model reached them?
Key AI Concepts

Common Misconceptions About AI

AI is not

  • A human mind inside a machine
  • Always correct or objective
  • Fully autonomous in most business contexts
  • A replacement for all jobs

AI is better understood as

  • A powerful pattern-recognition tool
  • A decision-support technology
  • A productivity enhancer
  • A system that still requires governance
Key insight: The real business challenge is not whether AI is intelligent — but how organisations integrate it responsibly into information systems.
AI Milestone

IBM Deep Blue and the Defeat of Garry Kasparov

Why Deep Blue mattered

In 1997, IBM’s Deep Blue defeated world chess champion Garry Kasparov in a historic match. This was one of the first moments when the public clearly saw that a machine could outperform a human expert in a highly respected intellectual game.

What made it powerful?

  • Deep Blue could evaluate huge numbers of possible chess positions very quickly.
  • It combined specialised hardware with advanced chess search techniques.
  • It did not “think” like a human, but it could calculate at extraordinary speed and depth.

Why this matters in AI history

  • It showed that computers could beat elite human decision-makers in narrow domains.
  • It raised public interest in artificial intelligence long before ChatGPT.
  • It reminds us that AI success often begins with specialised systems, not general intelligence.
Pause & Think: Deep Blue beat a world champion, but could it write an essay, drive a car, or hold a conversation? What does that tell us about the difference between narrow AI and AGI?

AI Milestone

AlphaGo and the Defeat of Human Go Champions

Why AlphaGo mattered

In 2016, DeepMind’s AlphaGo defeated South Korean Go champion Lee Sedol. In 2017, it also defeated Chinese world number one Ke Jie. These victories were especially significant because Go had long been seen as a game requiring intuition, pattern recognition, and creative judgement.

What made AlphaGo different?

  • It used deep neural networks to evaluate board positions.
  • It improved through self-play and reinforcement learning.
  • It did not rely only on brute force search in the way older systems did.

Why this matters in AI history

  • It marked a major leap beyond rule-based game-playing systems.
  • It showed that AI could learn highly complex strategies.
  • It helped shift public discussion from “Can machines calculate?” to “Can machines learn?”
Pause & Think: Deep Blue mainly showed the power of computation in a narrow task. AlphaGo showed the power of learning. Why is that an important step toward more capable AI systems? Alpha Go Doc

Strategic Shock

Why Google Panicked After ChatGPT

The threat

  • Google’s core business depends heavily on search.
  • ChatGPT showed that users might ask an AI assistant directly instead of searching through links.
  • This created fear that conversational AI could weaken Google’s most profitable gateway to the web.

Reported response

  • Google reportedly treated ChatGPT as a “code red” moment.
  • Leadership pushed teams to accelerate AI product work.
  • Google later reorganised major AI efforts more tightly around DeepMind.

Why this matters in business

  • Even dominant firms can be disrupted by interface change.
  • AI is not only a new tool — it can threaten existing revenue models.
  • When the user interface changes, the whole competitive landscape can change.
Discussion: Was ChatGPT mainly a better technology, or was it a better way for users to access information?
Pause & Think Why would Google risk damaging its own core business?

Because waiting can be more dangerous than self-disruption.

  • A rival may redefine the market first.
  • User habits can shift faster than large firms expect.
  • Protecting the present too aggressively can cost the future.
Strategy Lesson

Sometimes Firms Must Hurt Today’s Business to Survive Tomorrow

The classic problem

  • Successful firms build strong profitable business models.
  • New technologies may threaten those same models.
  • Leaders face a dilemma: protect current revenue or risk disruption.

Example: Camera manufacturers

  • Some firms were slow to adopt digital photography.
  • They feared destroying their profitable film businesses.
  • Competitors who embraced digital gained market leadership.

Connection to AI and Google

  • Conversational AI may reduce traditional search usage.
  • Google still accelerated AI development despite risk.
  • This reflects learning from past technology disruptions.
Discussion: Is it better to disrupt your own business or wait and react?
Strategic insight: Long-term survival sometimes requires firms to accept short-term losses or cannibalisation.
Industry Story

The Sam Altman Leadership Crisis (2023)

What happened?

  • OpenAI CEO Sam Altman was suddenly removed by the board.
  • Board cited communication and governance concerns.
  • Microsoft and employees reacted strongly.
  • Hundreds of staff threatened to resign.
  • Altman returned as CEO within days.

Why this shocked the tech world

  • Showed how fragile AI leadership structures were.
  • Raised questions about who controls powerful AI.
  • Highlighted tension between safety and commercial growth.

BIS perspective

  • Technology strategy depends on governance.
  • Leadership instability can affect entire ecosystems.
  • AI firms influence global business platforms.
Discussion: Should critical AI companies be run like startups or like public institutions?

Big Debate

AI Safety vs AI Acceleration

Safety-first camp

  • Move carefully with powerful AI systems
  • Focus on alignment, interpretability and control
  • Worry about misuse, bias, systemic harm and loss of oversight
  • Often associated with stronger governance and staged deployment

Acceleration camp

  • Move fast to build capability and scale
  • Emphasise innovation, competition and rapid product rollout
  • Argue that progress itself creates learning and economic value
  • Worry that slowing down may let rivals dominate

Why this split matters

Many major AI disagreements can be understood through this tension. Anthropic leaned hard into safety language. OpenAI has often tried to balance safety with fast commercial deployment. Musk has criticised what he sees as closed or captured AI systems while also racing to build a powerful competitor.

Think: If AI is too powerful to release casually, who should decide the pace of development?
Future Concept

What Is Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)?

Current AI

Specialised systems trained for specific tasks such as writing text, recognising images, or forecasting demand.

AGI vision

A future AI that can perform most intellectual tasks that humans can perform across many domains.

Debate

Experts disagree on when or whether AGI will be achieved — and how society should prepare.

Better explanation: AGI is not about starting with no knowledge. It is about being able to continually learn new knowledge and apply it flexibly across many different situations.
Business Information Systems

What This Means for Business Information Systems

ERP Systems

AI can support demand forecasting, procurement planning, anomaly detection and operational summarisation inside enterprise systems.

CRM Systems

AI can personalise communications, score leads, predict churn, summarise customer interactions and support service agents.

Finance Systems

AI can help with fraud detection, exception reporting, document review, forecasting and audit support.

HR Systems

AI can help with skills matching, workforce planning, employee helpdesks and training recommendations.

Decision Support

Executives increasingly expect AI-generated summaries, scenario comparisons and dashboard insights on top of ordinary reporting systems.

Governance Layer

The more AI enters business systems, the more firms need policy, accountability, audit trails, data quality controls and human review.

Teaching takeaway: Frontier AI rivalries may seem dramatic, but their real business impact shows up when these tools are embedded into everyday information systems.
